Rules and Regulations!

Rule No. 1: Court Dimensions & Equipment: Defines the 94x50 foot court, the 10-foot rim height, backboard lights for the time for both game time and possession time, and proper game equipment.

Rule No. 2: Duties of the Officials: Details the responsibilities of referees, scorers, and timers, including the authority of the lead referee and use of replay.

Rule No. 3: For Players, Substitutes & Coaches: Limits teams to 5 players on the court at one time, regulates substitution procedures, and defines roster requirements.

Rule No. 4: Definitions: Defines key terms: team possession, live/dead ball, 8-second backcourt rule, 3-second lane violation, and the restricted area.

Rule No. 5: Scoring and Timing: Sets games to four 12-minute quarters with 5-minute overtimes. Defines scoring by 3, 2, or 1 point and stopping the clock.

Rule No. 6: Putting Ball in Play & Live/Dead Ball: Controls jump balls, inbound requirements, and when the ball becomes live or is a dead ball.

Rule No. 7: Shot Clock: Mandates a 24-second shot clock that resets only if the ball hits the rim, providing offensive teams a time limit to score. If the offensive team shoots a shot, misses, and recovers the ball the shot clock resets to the default time of 14 seconds instead of the usual 24 seconds.

Rule No. 8: Out-of-Bounds and Throw-In: Defines a ball out-of-bounds when it touches the line, floor, or objects outside the boundary, awarding possession to the opposing team.

Rule No. 9: Free Throws and Penalties: When a player is fouled in the act of shooting or while their team is in the bonus they'll shoot between 1-4 free throws. Depending on the type of foul and the outcome the shooter has 10 seconds to shoot the free thow, and players must occupy specific lane spaces on the court.

Rule No. 10: Violations and Penalties: Defines illegal actions like traveling, double dribbling, carrying, 3-second violation on offnse and defense that result in a loss of possession.
